The model in the simulation makes use of various accelerations. Braking can be seen as acceleration as well, in that case the outcome is negative. The used numbers are calculated as following and based on various observations \cite{car-speeds}\cite{car-speeds-2}:

\bigskip
% 100 kph, use 3.6 to convert to ms
\DIVIDE{100}{3.6}{\msa}
v = $\frac{100kph}{3.6}=\msa ms$\\

% 2.6 s, time needed for 0-100
\def\time{2.6}
\DIVIDE{\msa}{\time}{\aa}
a = $\frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{\msa}{\time} = \aa ms^2$\\

% calculate distance for calculated acceleration and time
\SQUARE{\time}{\square}
\MULTIPLY{\aa}{\square}{\tmp}
\MULTIPLY{0.5}{\tmp}{\ma}
s = $\frac{1}{2} at^2 = \frac{1}{2} * \aa * \time^2 = \ma m $\\

% distance per second, linear accelerated
\DIVIDE{\ma}{\time}{\lms}
$\ma m / \time s = \lms m/s$\\

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\DIVIDE{200}{3.6}{\msb}
\def\time{2.6}
\SUBTRACT{\msb}{\msa}{\s}
\DIVIDE{\s}{\time}{\ab}
\SQUARE{\time}{\square}
\MULTIPLY{\ab}{\square}{\tmp}
\MULTIPLY{0.5}{\tmp}{\mb}
\DIVIDE{\mb}{\time}{\lms}
%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\DIVIDE{300}{3.6}{\msc}
\def\time{5.4}
\SUBTRACT{\msc}{\msb}{\s}
\DIVIDE{\s}{\time}{\ac}
\SQUARE{\time}{\square}
\MULTIPLY{\ac}{\square}{\tmp}
\MULTIPLY{0.5}{\tmp}{\mc}
\DIVIDE{\mc}{\time}{\lms}
